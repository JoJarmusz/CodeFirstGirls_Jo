Etude au bord de l'eau, 1864

Oil on canvas, 60.3 × 73 cm. Private collection.

Thanks to Guichard, the Morisot sisters met Camille Corot, with whom they painted on the banks of the Hauts de Seine. Corot opened the doors of the Salon to them in 1864, where they exhibited landscapes as amateurs. Then appeared the first criticism of a work by Berthe Morisot in the press: “the white fabrics that wrap the body of the young woman are very fine and warmly colored, the landscape is in true tone (…) painted with a lot of energy “.

Edma and Berthe Morisot's father built a workshop in the garden of the family home. We count Corot, but also Jules Ferry, the painters Alfred Stevens, Félix Bracquemond, Carolus-Duran, Pierre Puvis de Chavannes and Henri Fantin-Latour, among the regulars of the Tuesday dinner.
