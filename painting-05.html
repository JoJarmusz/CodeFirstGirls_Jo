Femme à sa toilette, 1875.

Oil on canvas, 60.3 × 80.4 cm. Art Institute – Chicago.

The focal point is the woman at her toilette. She’s positioned in a prominent spot around the center of the painting. The subject's looking off to the side at the mirror, unaware of our presence as the viewer

At the second Impressionist exhibition, which welcomed thousands of visitors to the Durand-Ruel gallery, Berthe Morisot presented sixteen works, notably Femme à sa toilette . The press deplores the incompleteness of his paintings. Impressionism's most ardent detractor, Albert Wolff, is not so categorical about it: “feminine grace is maintained amidst the excesses of a delirious spirit”.
