La Rivière de Pont Aven à Roz-Bras, 1867.

Oil on canvas, 55 × 73 cm. Private collection – Chicago.

The painting marks the beginning of Morisot's individual career and was officially exhibited at the 1868 Salon.

If Morisot has become “Madame Manet” for her friends, she continues to sign her works under her maiden name . Her notoriety and her social status lead her to open her salon on Thursdays, where she receives artistic and literary all-Parisians. Berthe Morisot regards Eva Gonzalès (former pupil and model of Manet) with mistrust, but she befriends Marie Bracquemond and Mary Cassatt, thanks to whom she will participate in two exhibitions at the Woman's Art Club in New York.
