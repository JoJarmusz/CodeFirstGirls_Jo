La Seine en aval du pont d’Iéna, 1866.

Oil on canvas, 51 × 73 cm. Private collection.

The theme was also painted by Paul Gauguin, although he went for the winter landscape.

The first impressionist exhibition took place in 1874, in the studio of the Nadar photographers. A week before the opening, Puvis de Chavannes had tried to convince Morisot of the fiasco of the enterprise, but she showed there fourteen oils, three pastels and three watercolours , including Le Berceau.
